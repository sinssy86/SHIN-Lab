SHIN Lab · Lehigh University College of Health

Research across S·H·I·N

SHIN Lab studies where people live, move, sleep, and recover. We combine spatial epidemiology, wearable and environmental sensing, and community-engaged research to understand how everyday environments shape sleep, physical activity, and well-being. The four letters of the lab's name — Space · Health · Interaction · Nature — are also its four research pillars.

Methods that travel across fields

Wearable and Mobile Health Measurement

Measuring sleep, physical activity, fatigue, stress, and daily behavior in real-world settings.

Spatial Epidemiology and Environmental Context

Linking GIS, environmental data, community audits, and activity spaces to population health.

Accessibility and Inclusive Spatial Design

Studying walkability, tactile mapping, and inclusive approaches to spatial information and navigation.

Community-Engaged Health Behavior Research

Working with students, community partners, and interdisciplinary teams to translate evidence into practical health strategies.

AI-Assisted Public Health Methods

Using computational and multimodal tools while attending to validity, bias, privacy, and equity.
